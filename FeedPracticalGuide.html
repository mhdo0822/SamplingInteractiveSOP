⚠️ Please follow the instructions below for collecting representative feed samples. Make sure to also get the photo of each feed.

Basic principles

  • Always aim for a representative sample.
  • Cover the potential range of maturity harvested and fed (e.g., early vegetative, late vegetative, early bloom, mid bloom, late bloom, milk stage, dough stage, mature).
  • If the species is harvested multiple times a year, sample across cuttings.
  • Where the species is produced under distinct climates/regions, collect per region.
  • Sample both dry (summer) and wet (winter) seasons where relevant.
  • Target ≈ 1 kg dried & ground material per ingredient (see table below).

Equipment

  • Forage sampler
  • 5-gallon/ 19-liter bucket
  • Hatchet/shear
  • Clipper
  • Sack thief (bag trier)
  • Grain probe
  • 30 x 30 cm and 50 x 50 cm frame

Distribution plan

  • Send ≥ 500 g dried & ground to CVAS (all samples).
  • For selected samples: 120 g to ILRI (NIR).
  • Store 200 g of samples as backup.

Labeling

  • Each samples gets an ID, a QR code, and a image.
  • ID format: [COUNTRY][SOURCE#][LAB] → e.g., IND1C, IND1I, IND1L, IND1B.
  • COUNTRY = 3-letter code (e.g., IND).
  • SOURCE# = sequential number for the feed source/lot (1, 2, 3…).
  • LAB = C (CVAS), I (ILRI), L (local lab).

Attention

The video demos are intended to give an idea of what to do and may slightly differ from the recommended protocols. Please make sure to follow the protocol steps exactly.

Hay / Straw

With hay probe (preferred)

  • Sample ≥20% of bales per lot (one core per randomly selected bale).
  • Square bales: insert the probe perpendicular on the small end.
  • Round bales: insert the probe around the curved circumference (not the end).
  • Penetrate ~30 cm.
  • Place each subsample in a ~19-liter bucket and mix to get a single composite representative sample.
  • Put the representative sample in a plastic bag and seal tightly.
Watch demo

Without hay probe

  • Sample ≥20% of bales per lot (one core per randomly selected bale).
  • Remove a small section from each bale and cut the hay into (~8 cm) lengths with shears or a hatchet.
  • Make sure to include a representative combination of leaves and stems. Placing samples on a smoother surface can be helpful to ensure leaves are collected.
  • Mix and randomly grab handfuls of the chopped representative sample.
  • Mix and put the representative sample in a plastic bag and seal tightly.
Watch demo

Loose hay/straw

  • Get samples from 15–20 positions per lot; keep all leafy material.
  • Cut the hay into 3 in (≈8 cm).
  • Mix and randomly grab handfuls of the chopped representative sample.
  • Mix and put the representative sample in a plastic bag and seal tightly.

Chopped or ground hay

  • During grinding: collect ~10 grab samples across the lot, combine in one plastic bag, mix, seal tightly.
  • Previously ground/chopped piles: sample beneath the surface; take ~¼ of samples from the top half and the rest from the lower half; avoid fines sifting between fingers. Mix in a plastic bag and seal tightly.

Pasture

  • Walk an "M" pattern across the pasture and harvest at regular intervals.
  • Collect from 8–10 random sites within areas of similar species, soils, and topography.
  • At each site, clip at 5 cm stubble height (above soil). Use a 30.5 × 30.5 cm (1 ft²) frame; use 50 × 50 cm if forage density/structure requires it.
  • Combine and mix all cut forage in a clean container, then take a representative subsample for analysis.
  • Put the representative sample in a plastic bag and seal tightly.
Watch demo

Forage crops (standing field)

  • Walk an "M" pattern and harvest at regular intervals.
  • Collect from 8–10 random sites across the field.
  • At each site, cut all plants within a 1 ft² area (≈30.5 × 30.5 cm) at normal harvesting height.
  • Chop to 1–2 in (2.5–5 cm), mix thoroughly, and take a representative subsample.
  • Put the representative sample in a plastic bag and seal tightly.

Green‑chopped forages

  • Randomly grab a handful of the chopped material from the cutter, blower or several locations in a wagon or truck.
  • Tightly pack the bag and seal after excluding the air.

Silage

General

  • Collect from multiple locations to be representative.
  • Freeze immediately after collection and keep frozen until processing.

Trench silo

  • Clean the face (center); remove a 6 × 12 in (15 × 30 cm) column from top to bottom, mix thoroughly, take a representative subsample.
  • Drill 4–6 holes from the top (post-hole digger or similar), combine only good silage, mix, and subsample.

Upright silo

  • Representative sampling is difficult.
  • Take grab samples from a day's feed; enter only if fermentation has subsided (safety first).
  • Use the same approach as an unopened trench (multiple top holes, mix, subsample).

Silo bag

  • Take 5–8 handfuls across the entire face, mix in a bucket, and bag a representative sample.
  • Collect 8–10 core samples along the entire length and both sides; tape core holes closed to prevent oxygen entry/spoilage. Mix and bag a representative sample.

Sacked feed

  • From ≥5–6 sacks, take 1–2 lb (0.45–0.90 kg) total, mix, and submit a representative subsample.
  • Use a small probe or sack thief when available.

Concentrates

Bulk/commodity feeds

  • Composite of at least 10 to 15 areas of a given lot of feed.
  • When mixing the composite, avoid particle-size segregation to prevent bias.

Grain in bin

  • Use a grain probe to collect samples when possible.
  • If no probe is available, take random grab samples from 10–15 areas, combine and mix, and submit a representative subsample for analysis.

Coproduct feeds

  • For each load received, take grab samples from 4–5 locations.
  • Composite and place in a properly sealed sample bag.

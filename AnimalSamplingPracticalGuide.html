Body weight (BW)

  • Weigh on the set schedule: day 0, then every 7 days (e.g., 0, 7, 14, 21) through the end of period 3.
  • Always weigh before morning feed.
  • Before each weighing, verify scale accuracy with a known reference weight.
  • Bring the cow to the platform; wait for the read to stabilize.
  • Record the stable value.

Ingredient dry matter (DM)

  • Collect a representative sample for each ingredient offered to animals.
  • Use a lab scale to weigh the sample.
  • Make sure to tare the container before weighing the sample.
  • Record sample fresh sample weight.
  • Dry the sample according to the specific method used (e.g. Forced circulation oven (preferred); Microwave oven; Air fryer; Koster tester).
  • Reweigh the sample to get the dry weight.
  • Compute the DM% (dry sample weight / fresh sample weight) * 100.
  • Use the DM result to adjust the diet.

Milk sampling

  • Homogenize the milk produced by the cow (bucket) to get a representative sample.
  • Fill the sampler vial (see lab specifications)).
  • Add preservative if specified.
  • Label the vial appropriately.
  • Place the vial into a cooler with ice packs while collecting other samples.
  • Refrigerate until shipment.

Urine sampling

  • Get a Jar/container.
  • Gently massage the perineum (just below the vulva) with short, repeated strokes until she urinates.
  • Collect the urine into the jar/container.
  • Filter to remove any dirt or debris.
  • Proceed with the required preparation/preservation.
  • Place the sample in a cooler with ice packs while collecting other samples.
  • Freeze immediately.

Feces sampling

  • If voluntary, collect fresh grab sample at the time of defecation.
  • If not voluntary, collect a sample from the animal rectum:
  • Wearing palpation gloves, gently insert a finger and then the hand into the rectum.
  • Grab a hanfull of feces.
  • Place the sample into a drying tray.
  • Dry immediately.

Methane emission — GreenFeed

  • Sampling design: 8 sampling time points over 3 consecutive days to represent every 3 h across a 24 h cycle.
  • Sampling times:
    • Day 1: 0900, 1500, 2100 h
    • Day 2: 0300, 1200, 1800 h
    • Day 3: 0000, 0600 h
  • Measurement event settings: GreenFeed dispenses 5 drops of pellets (approximately 36 g per drop) at 30 second intervals to ensure a 5 minute continuous measurement.
  • Background air and washout: Between sampling events, GreenFeed collects background air for a 2 minute washout to re-equilibrate to ambient gas concentrations.
  • If a cow does not voluntarily visit within the assigned time block, she may be encouraged with a small quantity of grain to maintain balanced sampling frequency across cows and treatments.
  • Calibration: Calibrate all GreenFeed units daily using the manufacturer’s auto calibration system.
